3D interaction prototype

Submerged

Submerged is an experimental prototype for a survival horror computer game, which uses 3D interaction technology. I contributed to the idea generation, design and final development of the prototype.

The prototype is developed in Unity and uses a 3D stereo cinema, a 360 degree sound system, a wireless controller (made by MaKey MaKey and LittleBits components), a telephone connection between two phones, and gyroscope and accelerometer data from a smartphone.

This allowed two players to collaborate with each their sensuous input and output.

The field of view is controlled by a player, who is blindfolded, while the other player have visuals. The two players have to collaborate in order to survive the monsters of the deep sea, slowly swimming towards them. The blindfolded player carries a controller, that has to be aimed precisely in order to shoot the monster. For this, the visuals come in handy.
