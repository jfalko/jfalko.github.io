Student Interaction Design Research

Conflict Minerals: Articulating Cultural and Societal Issues by Analyzing through a Critical Design Perspective

I wrote a paper for the annual Student Interaction Design Research conference held in Malm√∏ in April 2016. The paper was peer reviewed and accepted for the conference. At the conference, I presented my paper for the audience and answered questions afterwards.
The paper is about critical design, a field in interaction design, and how the perspective of critical design can be used to articulate serious societal, ethical and cultural problems, such as the conflict minerals used in electronics. For this, I analyzed three objects as critical designs.

Comments from reviewers:
"The author chose very good examples of critical designs which clearly communicate the problem of conflict minerals"
"In general the paper is interesting and thought provoking."
"I believe this paper is a good contribution for SIDeR"
