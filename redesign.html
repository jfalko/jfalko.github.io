Jeanette Falk

Postdoc at Aarhus University

jfo@edu.au.dk

My research interest is how the design and development of technology may be democratised. My work cover the topics and fields of interaction design, Human Computer Interaction (HCI), game design, and creativity. With a point of departure in predominantly pragmatist design process theory and qualitative research methodology, I have specialised in the study of the processual aspects of design and game design, most recently with a focus on hackathon and game jam formats. I strive for a close connection and exploration between theory and practice in my work, and have therefore, in addition to my research skills, obtained a broad range of hands-on skills in among other things design, prototyping, fabrication, and programming.

Cobots

This game was developed during Nordic Game Jam 2016 under the theme "Leak". We had 48 hours to develop the game.

I contributed to and facilitated the idea generation. During the game jam I sketched several ideas for levels and game mechanics. I worked in Unity, where I build the game from graphics assets and prefabs.

Rabbit Fire - Best Audio

The game was developed during Arsenalets Spring Jam 2016, which had the theme: "Cheating at the Nordic Game Jam".
During the game jam I contributed to the code of the game. Our game won the "Best Audio"-award. We had 72 hours to develop the game.

Clockwork Dream - Graduation Game

In the Autumn of 2015, I was accepted at the National Academy of Digital Interactive Entertainment, DADIU, as a level designer. As part of the graduation programme my team produced the tablet game, Clockwork Dream.
I designed and implemented the levels in accordance with the gameplay, flow as well as the player’s aesthetic experience based on criteria created by the Game Director, Art Director, and Game Designer.

Icescape

I designed and implemented the levels in accordance with the gameplay, flow as well as the player’s aesthetic experience based on criteria created by the Game Director, Art Director, and Game Designer.

Sheerboard - 1st prize winner

My team developed the concept for a design competition during a weekend, January 2014. The case was given by Ditmer and after winning the competition and presenting the concept for the audience and jury, my team were invited to present the concept for the rest of the Ditmer company.

The concept was about making the organizational structure in a small or medium-sized company clear and giving individual users an overview of their company's structure, while being simple and easy to use.
Our solution was the Sheerboard-concept, an automated and interactive visualization tool.

Speed Hackers - Semifinalist Winner

The game was developed during the Nordic Game Jam 2014 with the theme: Privacy. We won the semifinal rounds. I contributed to the game design, level design and play testing of the game.

Speed Hackers is a mobile multiplayer racing game. Two players race against each other to reach the finish line first, in order to hack the other player. While racing, the players have to avoid firewalls and choose the right portals to go through.

World Jump - Exhibited at the Storm P Museum, Copenhagen

As part of a summer course at Aarhus University, my team and I developed the game World Jump during August, 2014.
The game is for 4 players. The players control the game by high fiving each other, one pair for controlling jumps and the other pair for controlling the speed of the level. This is done by letting each player hold a wire connected to a MaKey MaKey. The purpose of the game was to encourage cooperation between the players.

Pulse Sensor Augmented Horror Game - Bachelor Project

For my bachelor project, I developed a horror survival game inspired by the game, Slender Man. The project was graded 12.

Via a pulse sensor connected to the player's ear lobe, the player's heart rate provides bio feedback to regulate the difficulty of the game. This was to investigate how a desktop computer game can be a bodily experience like eg. a Kinect game.

Taburban - Honourable Mention

Taburban is an app-concept, that was developed under the 24 hour design competition for the Student Interaction Design Research Conference in 2013 with the theme: empowering interactions.
Our concept received an honorable mention from the jury and were in the running until the last moments of the competition.

I contributed to the idea generation, and development of the show case video.
